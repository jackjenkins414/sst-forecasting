Transformer Experiment Summary
==============================
Dataset:
- NOAA OISST v2.1 daily SST
- Region: Coral Sea (140 E to 170 E, 25 S to 5 S)
- Date range: 1981-09-01 to 2000-12-31
- Grid: 81 lat x 121 lon (0.25 deg resolution)
- Ocean points after land mask: 7890

Preprocessing:
- Z-scored SST anomalies (climatology subtracted, divided by training std)
- Climatology and norm stats computed from training years only (no leakage)
- norm_mean = 0.00000, norm_std = 0.70023

Sliding windows:
- Input length: 90 days
- Forecast horizon: 7 days
- Input shape per sample: (90, 1, 81, 121)
- Target shape per sample: (7, 81, 121)

Split:
- Train: 1981-09-01 to 1995-12-31  (322 batches)
- Val:   1996-01-01 to 1998-12-31  (63 batches)
- Test:  1999-01-01 to 2000-12-31  (40 batches)
- Batch size: 16

Model:
- Encoder-only spatial flat Transformer (built from scratch)
- Components: SpatialProjection -> PositionalEncoding -> Encoder -> OutputHead
- d_model: 128
- Encoder blocks: 2
- Attention heads: 4
- FFN hidden dim (d_ff): 512
- Dropout: 0.1
- Total parameters: 10,500,735

Training:
- Optimiser: Adam, lr=1e-3, weight_decay=1e-4
- Gradient clipping: 1.0
- Loss: masked MSE over ocean cells, computed in normalised space
- Epochs: 8
- Device: CPU (M3 Pro MacBook)
- Final train loss: 0.5250 | Final val loss: 0.7175

Comparison to LSTM (StackedSpatialLSTM, 8 epochs):
                   Day 1   Day 7   Mean
- Persistence       0.33    0.70   0.58
- LSTM              0.60    0.63   0.62
- Transformer       0.49    0.61   0.56

Conclusion:
- The Transformer beats both the LSTM and persistence on mean RMSE.
- Per-horizon curve degrades naturally (0.49 -> 0.61), unlike the LSTMwhich was flat (0.60 -> 0.63)
- Beats persistence at every horizon from day 3 onwards. 
- Validation loss had not plateaued at 8 epochs (still trending down)
- Next stage: build a Temporal Fusion Transformer fork with per-horizon learned queries
 and cross-attention decoding, replacing the last-token decoding strategy that limits longer-horizon accuracy.
